Drift Prevention

Rules For Codex, Claude, And Humans

This is the formula to follow when creating new portal screens, curriculum pages, handouts, decks, or blueprints.

Do

  • Use the black logo on white for normal headers.
  • Use warm paper instead of a gray page background.
  • Use clay for primary action, progress, section labels, and selected states.
  • Reserve sage for key concepts and milestones.
  • Use the four callout types consistently.
  • Define light and dark versions for reusable components and icons.
  • Keep portal screens operational, not marketing-page styled.

Do Not

  • Do not bring back the cold gray background from the earlier draft.
  • Do not repeat the logo or full brand lockup multiple times on one screen.
  • Do not let clay become muddy or heavy; keep the canvas light.
  • Do not use sage as a generic decoration; it means important.
  • Do not invent new status meanings when a semantic color already exists.
  • Do not ship dark-mode components as an afterthought.
  • Do not nest cards inside cards or add decorative panels without purpose.
